**Gilly the Helpful Ghost**

In the little town of Maple Button, there lived a friendly ghost named Gilly.

Gilly the ghost did not say “Boo!” to scare people. Gilly the ghost said “Boo!” to say hello. Sometimes Gilly said it too quietly, because Gilly was a shy ghost.

Every night, when the moon rose over Maple Button, Gilly the ghost floated through the village looking for someone to help.

One evening, Gilly the ghost found a kitten stuck in a tree.

“Boo?” said Gilly the ghost gently.

The kitten meowed.

Gilly the ghost floated up beside the kitten and made a soft ghostly breeze. The leaves rustled, the branch dipped, and the kitten climbed safely down.

“Thank you, ghost!” said the kitten, which surprised Gilly very much.

The next night, Gilly the ghost found Mrs. Pepperpot searching for her lost spectacles.

“Boo?” said Gilly the ghost.

“Oh, hello, little ghost,” said Mrs. Pepperpot. “Have you seen my glasses?”

Gilly the ghost floated through the room and found them on top of Mrs. Pepperpot’s head.

“Well done, ghost!” she laughed.

Soon everyone in Maple Button knew about Gilly the friendly ghost. Children waved to the ghost. Dogs wagged their tails at the ghost. Even the mayor gave the ghost a tiny silver badge that said: Helpful Ghost.

From then on, Gilly the ghost was not shy anymore.

Every night, Gilly floated over Maple Button, glowing softly in the moonlight.

And whenever someone needed help, they listened for a gentle little voice saying:

“Boo?”
