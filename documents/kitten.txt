**The Kitten and the Wobbly Cake**

In a sunny little kitchen, there lived a mischievous kitten.

The kitten was small, soft, and very curious. The kitten liked shoelaces, paper bags, curtain tassels, and anything that rolled under the sofa. But most of all, the kitten liked trouble.

One morning, Grandma placed a tall pink cake on the kitchen table.

“This cake is for tea,” said Grandma. “No paws, little kitten.”

The kitten blinked sweetly.

Then the kitten waited.

When Grandma went to the garden, the kitten jumped onto a chair. Then the kitten jumped onto the table. Then the kitten padded around the cake very slowly.

The cake smelled like strawberries.

The kitten sniffed.

The kitten sneezed.

The cake wobbled.

“Oh no,” thought the kitten, though the kitten did not look very sorry.

The kitten tried to steady the cake with one paw. That made the cake wobble more. The kitten tried with two paws. That made pink icing stick to the kitten’s whiskers.

Just then, Grandma came back.

The kitten froze. The cake leaned left. The kitten leaned right. The cake leaned right. The kitten leaned left.

At last, the cake flopped gently onto a plate of sandwiches.

Grandma stared.

The kitten sat in the middle of the table with pink icing on its nose.

“You mischievous kitten,” said Grandma.

The kitten gave one tiny mew.

Grandma sighed, then laughed. “Well, now we have cake sandwiches.”

At tea time, everyone giggled about the kitten. The kitten sat proudly under the table, licking icing from one paw.

And by bedtime, the kitten had already found a new adventure: a ball of wool, a laundry basket, and a very wobbly lamp.
