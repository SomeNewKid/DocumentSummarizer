**Dibble the Little Devil**

Dibble was a little devil who lived under a red mushroom at the edge of Buttercup Wood.

Now, Dibble was not a scary devil. Dibble was an impish devil, which meant he liked tricks, giggles, and muddy footprints. Every morning, the little devil polished his tiny horns, tied his spotted scarf, and looked for harmless mischief.

One day, Dibble the devil crept into the village bakery. He sprinkled cinnamon on the bread, drew smiley faces in the flour, and turned all the buns upside down.

“Oh, that devil!” laughed Baker Bell. “Dibble the devil has visited again.”

Next, the little devil tiptoed to the schoolyard. Dibble the devil hid behind the slide and made the skipping ropes wiggle like sleepy snakes. The children laughed and shouted, “Hello, devil! We know it’s you!”

Dibble puffed up proudly. Being a funny devil was very satisfying.

But then Dibble saw Molly Mouse crying beside a puddle.

“My paper boat sank,” said Molly.

Dibble the devil scratched his head. Tricks were easy. Helping was trickier.

So Dibble used his pointy tail to fold a leaf into a boat. Then the little devil blew a warm puff of air, and the leaf boat sailed across the puddle.

Molly clapped. “Thank you, Dibble. You are a kind devil.”

Dibble blushed all the way to his horns.

From that day on, Dibble the devil still played silly tricks. He still made buns upside down and ropes wiggle.

But whenever someone needed help, Dibble the little devil helped first.

And that was the best trick of all.
